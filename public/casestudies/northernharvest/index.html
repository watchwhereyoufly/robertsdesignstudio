What I Inherited

Nothing.

No website. No active Instagram. No brand guidelines. No organized asset library. No sales collateral. No consistent packaging. No content pipeline. A founder under pressure from his distributor, and a VP of Sales walking into 250+ dispensaries empty-handed.

The only things that existed: a logo, a loose color palette, and incredible cannabis.
Approach

Heritage brand, not cannabis culture.

I positioned Northern Harvest as a premium heritage brand, not a typical cannabis company. The product quality demanded it. The distributor's feedback confirmed the gap was perception, not product.

Positioning

Premium Heritage

Reference points: Johnnie Walker (premium, tiered, legacy), Wyld (nature aesthetic done right), REI, Patagonia. Understated confidence. Earned, not boasted.

Decision Framework

The Million Dollar Test

"Is this how a million-dollar-per-month brand would present itself?" If yes, ship it. If no, redo it. Every decision ran through this filter.

Speed

Field First

The sales team needed materials yesterday. I prioritized revenue-generating collateral over internal documentation. Menus and sell sheets shipped in week three. Brand guidelines came after.

Systems

Scale Beyond Me

I built systems that outlast my involvement. Automated menu workflows, organized asset libraries, documented brand rules. A brand that runs without needing a designer for every update.

Reflection

What I would do differently.

With more time, I would have pushed for a professional photography shoot before building the website. The product deserves it. What surprised me is how much the "Million Dollar Test" simplified decision-making. One filter eliminated hours of debate. When every stakeholder is aligned on a single question, the work moves faster than any process framework.

This project is ongoing. The brand is still evolving. But the foundation is built, and it is built to scale.
